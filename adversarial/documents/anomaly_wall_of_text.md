We operate in a highly competitive and rapidly changing environment. New risk factors emerge from time to time, and it is not possible for management to predict all such risk factors, nor can we assess the impact of all such risk factors on our business or the extent to which any factor or combination of factors may cause actual results to differ materially from those contained in any forward-looking statements. We have a limited history of profitable operations and our revenue and income are difficult to forecast. We may experience significant fluctuations in our results of operations, making it difficult for you to predict our future performance. Our quarterly results may fluctuate significantly and may not fully reflect the underlying performance of our business due to, among other factors, the timing of transactions and customer behavior with respect to new service subscriptions, the timing of recognition of revenue, the impact of seasonality on our business, the timing of significant new product or service announcements by us or our competitors, and general economic, industry, and market conditions. We are dependent on the economic health of our current and prospective customers, and if the industries in which we operate experience a downturn, our business and results of operations may be adversely affected. Many of our customers are in industries that are particularly sensitive to economic downturns, and any deterioration in the financial condition of our customers or the industries in which they operate could negatively affect the demand for our products and services, increase credit risk, and impair our ability to collect accounts receivable. Our revenue growth depends on our ability to attract and retain customers, expand our relationships with existing customers, and enter into agreements with new customers. If we are unable to attract new customers or expand our relationships with existing customers, our revenue will not grow as expected and our results of operations would be adversely affected. We may not be able to compete successfully against current and future competitors. The markets in which we operate are highly competitive, rapidly evolving, fragmented, and subject to shifting customer needs and frequent introductions of new products and services by our competitors. Many of our current competitors have, and potential new competitors may have, greater resources, more established customer bases, greater name recognition, and a broader range of products and services than we have. Our competitors may be able to respond more quickly to new or changing opportunities, technologies, and customer requirements. Competitive pressures could result in increased pricing pressure, reduced profit margins, increased sales and marketing expenses, and failure to increase or maintain our market share, any of which could adversely affect our operating results. We may experience difficulties in managing our growth. We have experienced and may continue to experience rapid growth in our business, and this growth has placed, and may continue to place, significant demands on our management, financial, and other resources. If we are unable to effectively manage our growth, our financial performance may be adversely affected and our employees, customers, and other constituents may be harmed. We have made and expect to continue to make acquisitions and investments. The identification of suitable acquisition candidates is difficult, the competition for suitable targets is intense, and acquisitions involve numerous risks, including the diversion of management attention from other business concerns, the disruption of the acquired company's business, the potential loss of key personnel of the acquired company, difficulties in integrating the operations, technology, products, and services of the acquired company, assumption of contingent and other liabilities, and impairment of acquired intangible assets and goodwill. Our business involves the collection, processing, and storage of sensitive and confidential information belonging to our customers, which subjects us to regulatory compliance obligations and risk of liability in the event of a breach of our security measures or unintended disclosure of information. We have invested and will continue to invest in our security infrastructure and practices; however, there can be no assurance that these measures will prevent breaches or unauthorized access to our systems. Any such breach could result in significant legal exposure and liability, damage to our reputation, and a loss of confidence in our security measures, which could have a material adverse effect on our business. We are subject to a wide variety of laws and regulations domestically and internationally, including privacy laws, anti-corruption laws, consumer protection laws, intellectual property laws, employment laws, environmental laws, and other regulations, the violation of which could expose us to significant monetary penalties and other sanctions. The regulatory environment in which we operate is complex and continues to evolve. New laws and regulations, or the application of existing laws and regulations to our business, may impose significant compliance obligations, costs, and restrictions. Compliance with these obligations may require significant management time and attention and may divert resources from other aspects of our business. We are currently, and from time to time will be, involved in litigation, claims, and other legal proceedings that arise in the ordinary course of our business and that could adversely affect our business. An unfavorable outcome in any such matter could have a material adverse effect on our business, financial condition, and results of operations. We rely on highly skilled personnel and the continued service of our key technical and senior management personnel. The loss of the services of any of our key personnel, the inability to retain and attract additional key personnel, or any succession planning failures could affect our ability to pursue our growth strategy and maintain the quality of our products and services. We are incorporated in Delaware and our principal place of business is in the State of New York. Our business is subject to taxation in multiple jurisdictions and changes in tax rates or tax legislation could adversely affect our effective tax rate. In addition, we may be subject to additional tax obligations in jurisdictions in which we have not previously had a tax presence. We have a significant amount of intellectual property which is important to our competitive position, and our failure to protect this intellectual property could result in the loss of competitive advantages. Our ability to protect our intellectual property depends in part on our success in obtaining and maintaining patents and other intellectual property rights, the strength and enforceability of our patents, the scope of patent protection, our ability to defend ourselves against infringement claims by third parties, and our ability to prevent others from misappropriating our trade secrets. We make our products available through the internet and rely on internet infrastructure that we do not own or control and that may be subject to disruption or damage. We depend on third-party technology and services, including cloud computing services, network services, data services, financial services, and specialized software providers, and any disruption in these services could harm our ability to serve our customers. Climate change may have a long-term adverse impact on our business and results of operations. Various experts have expressed concern that climate change will impose increasing regulatory, reputational, and operational risks on businesses. We have operations in locations that may be susceptible to the impact of climate change, including extreme weather events such as floods, hurricanes, and droughts. Although we have implemented various plans and programs to manage these risks, we may not be able to fully mitigate the effects of extreme weather events or other climate-related impacts, which could adversely affect our business. We are subject to various government export controls, economic sanctions, and other laws and regulations that may restrict our ability to conduct business with certain individuals and entities, and may expose us to liability for any violations. Our products and services may be subject to U.S. export controls and sanctions programs administered by the Office of Foreign Assets Control of the U.S. Department of the Treasury and the Bureau of Industry and Security of the U.S. Department of Commerce. If we fail to comply with these laws, we could suffer civil and criminal penalties, including large monetary fines, and our products could be denied export privileges.
