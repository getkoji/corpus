# Grandma's Apple Pie Recipe

**Prep time:** 20 minutes
**Cook time:** 50 minutes
**Servings:** 8

## Ingredients

- 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
- 1 tsp salt
- 1 cup cold butter, cubed
- 6 tbsp ice water
- 8 cups thinly sliced apples (Granny Smith work best)
- 3/4 cup granulated sugar
- 2 tbsp lemon juice
- 1 tsp cinnamon
- 1/4 tsp nutmeg
- 2 tbsp butter

## Instructions

1. Combine flour and salt in a large bowl. Cut in cold butter until
   the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Add ice water gradually,
   mixing until dough just comes together. Divide in half, wrap in
   plastic, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.

2. Preheat oven to 425°F. Roll out one portion of dough and line a
   9-inch pie plate.

3. In a large bowl, toss apples with sugar, lemon juice, cinnamon,
   and nutmeg. Pour filling into the pie crust and dot with butter.

4. Roll out the second portion of dough and place over the filling.
   Trim and crimp the edges. Cut several slits in the top crust to
   allow steam to escape.

5. Bake for 50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the
   filling is bubbling through the slits. Cool on a wire rack before
   serving.

## Notes

Serve warm with vanilla ice cream for a classic dessert. The recipe
has been in our family for four generations and originated in
Pennsylvania Dutch country.
